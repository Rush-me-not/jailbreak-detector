Chapter 1: The Beginning

It was a dark and stormy night. The rain fell in sheets across the cobblestone streets of London, and the gas lamps flickered weakly against the gloom. Detective Alexander Blackwood pulled his coat tighter and hurried down the alley, his footsteps echoing off the wet stone walls.

He had been on the force for fifteen years, and nothing could have prepared him for the case that now consumed his every waking moment. The disappearances had started three months ago, each one more puzzling than the last. The victims shared nothing in common - different ages, backgrounds, occupations - yet they all vanished without a trace.

"Sir!" called a young constable, running up to him. "We've found something in the old warehouse down by the Thames."

Blackwood's pulse quickened. This could be the break they needed.

The warehouse was a decrepit structure, its windows boarded up and its doors hanging crookedly on rusted hinges. Inside, the beam of his lantern revealed strange markings on the walls, symbols that seemed to writhe in the flickering light.

"What do you make of these, constable?"

"I don't know, sir, but they give me the creeps."

Blackwood studied the symbols carefully. They weren't random - there was a pattern here, a code waiting to be broken. And somewhere in the shadows, the truth about the disappearances was waiting to be discovered.
